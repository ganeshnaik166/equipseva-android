Why KYC matters before any engineer enters your hospital.

A hospital is a sensitive environment — patients, drugs, records. The person walking into the OT to fix a light is, in effect, on staff for the duration of that visit. Verification matters.

Trust on a hospital floor is layered. There's the trust between surgeon and patient, between nurse and procurement, between procurement and supplier. The trust most procurement teams under-invest in is the one between hospital and field engineer — the person who shows up with a tool kit, gets escorted to the OT, and has thirty minutes alone with a ₹50-lakh machine.

What "KYC" really means in 2026

For EquipSeva, every engineer must clear three checks before they can bid on a single job:

  • Aadhaar OTP. Validates the engineer's identity against the UIDAI registry.
  • PAN check. Cross-references their tax identity. Pseudonyms fail here.
  • Live selfie liveness. Confirms the human submitting the docs is the same human in the documents — not someone holding up a photo.

Why platforms without KYC expose hospitals to risk

When a marketplace is "open" — anyone can sign up and bid — the cost of identity arbitrage falls onto the hospital. Repeated incidents in low-verification platforms across other industries (last-mile delivery, ride hailing) show the same pattern: the platform scales fast, the verified engineers are slowly outnumbered by anonymous ones, and trust erodes job by job until hospitals stop using the platform entirely.

"The first thing our biomedical lead asks is 'is the engineer verified?' If the answer is anything but yes, the conversation is over."

What hospitals should expect

A serious platform will give your procurement team:

  1. A verified profile photo and full legal name on every bid.
  2. The engineer's KYC status visible in-app before acceptance.
  3. An audit trail of every job the engineer has closed on the platform.
  4. A dispute path that doesn't depend on tracking down the engineer's WhatsApp.

Privacy is not the opposite of verification

A common misconception is that KYC equals "data exposure." It doesn't. EquipSeva encrypts KYC documents at rest, restricts access to verification and dispute resolution staff, and supports one-tap account deletion (with KYC document purge) inside the engineer app. Verification and privacy are complements, not opposites.

The bottom line

Hospitals don't need a faster way to find unverified engineers. They need a faster way to find verified ones. That's the entire reason EquipSeva exists.
